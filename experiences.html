02
Whale Watching

Humpbacks, Orcas &
Marine Wildlife

The Inside Passage is one of the most reliable places on earth to observe humpback whales in the wild. During the summer feeding season, pods of humpbacks gather to bubble-net feed — a complex cooperative hunting behavior that is genuinely breathtaking to witness from fifty feet away on the deck of your private yacht.

Beyond whales, the waters around Juneau host orca pods, Dall's porpoise, Steller sea lions, harbor seals, and an extraordinary density of bald eagles. Every trip into the passage is a wildlife encounter waiting to happen.

Species Humpback Whales, Orca, Dall's Porpoise, Steller Sea Lions, Harbor Seals, Bald Eagles
Best Season June through September
Viewing From your private vessel — no crowded tour boats, no strangers

Glacier Bay & Scenic Cruising

Ancient Ice & the
Grand Inside Passage

Glacier Bay National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most dramatic landscapes on the planet — 3.3 million acres of tidewater glaciers, fjords, and wilderness that has only been ice-free since the 1700s. Cruising into Glacier Bay on a private yacht, with no tour schedule and no crowds, is a genuinely once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Beyond Glacier Bay, the Inside Passage offers hundreds of miles of fjords, ancient rainforest coastline, uninhabited islands, and coastal villages with rich Tlingit heritage to explore at your own pace.

FAQ

Common Questions

Do I need fishing experience to book a fishing charter?
Not at all. Steve and the crew are happy to walk first-time anglers through everything — from rigging to setting the hook to landing a fish. Add a skippered captain for the full guided experience.
Can I combine multiple experiences on one charter?
Absolutely — that's the beauty of a private charter. You might fish in the morning, watch whales in the afternoon, and set a crab pot at anchor for dinner. Steve will help you build an itinerary that fits your time and interests.
What's the difference between bareboat and skippered?
A bareboat charter means you captain the vessel yourself — Alaska boating experience is required. A skippered charter adds Steve or one of our licensed captains at $650/day. For most guests, skippered is the right choice: you get local knowledge, safety expertise, and the freedom to focus entirely on the experience.
How far in advance should I book?
As early as possible. Summer months — especially July and August — book out months in advance. We recommend contacting Steve by late winter or early spring to secure your preferred dates for the upcoming season.
What happens if the weather is bad?
Southeast Alaska weather is dynamic but the Inside Passage is generally well-protected from open ocean swells. Steve has decades of experience reading local conditions and knows when to go, when to wait, and where to find shelter. Your safety always comes first.
Do I need to bring my own food and fuel?
Yes — provisions and fuel are the client's responsibility. This allows you to stock exactly what you like. We can advise on quantities based on your trip length. A convenience package ($45/person) covers all disposable supplies: paper goods, toiletries, and cleaning supplies.
Is Glacier Bay accessible on a shorter charter?
Glacier Bay is best suited to multi-day charters of 3 days or more given the travel time from Juneau. For day trips, there are spectacular glaciers and wildlife much closer to Auke Bay Harbor that make for equally memorable single-day adventures.
What should I bring on my charter?
Layers are essential — Alaska weather changes fast. Waterproof outerwear, rubber-soled non-marking shoes, sunscreen, polarized sunglasses, and your Alaska Sport Fishing License (if fishing). Bring a camera with plenty of storage. Check the Travel Prep Links page for a full packing guide.
