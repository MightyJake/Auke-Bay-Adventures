Foundation

Mission & Positioning

Mission Statement

To provide unforgettable private yacht charter experiences on Alaska's Inside Passage — where the wilderness of Southeast Alaska becomes accessible on your terms, with world-class service and vessels built for the region.

Brand Positioning

Maritime Luxury meets Rugged Alaska. Auke Bay Adventures is not a cruise line or a tour operator — it's a private key to the most spectacular and remote wilderness in North America, delivered with genuine expertise and personal warmth.

Brand Personality

Authoritative Adventurous Warm Genuine Luxurious Trustworthy

Target Audience

Experienced anglers, adventure travelers, nature photographers, families seeking bucket-list Alaska experiences, and corporate/group charter clients. Typically 35–65, affluent, value authenticity over mass tourism.

Identity

Logo Usage

The Auke Bay Adventures logo is the primary brand identifier. Use it consistently across all materials. The logo should always have clear space equal to the height of the "A" in Adventures on all sides.

✓ Primary — On Navy

Preferred. Use on all dark navy backgrounds.

✓ Acceptable — On Ocean Blue

Works well on mid-tone blues.

✓ Acceptable — On Cream/Light

Use on light backgrounds with sufficient contrast.

Logo Don'ts

  • Do not stretch, squish, or distort the logo proportions
  • Do not apply drop shadows or glows directly to the logo
  • Do not place the logo over busy photographs without a dark overlay
  • Do not change the logo colors — use the official asset only
  • Do not make the logo smaller than 36px tall in digital contexts
  • Do not rotate or flip the logo orientation

An 8px-base spacing scale keeps layouts consistent and visually harmonious. Major sections use larger spacing units for breathing room.

Section padding: 80–120px top/bottom on desktop, 48–64px on mobile. Horizontal padding scales from 24px (mobile) to 48px+ (desktop). The max content width is ~1440px. Page gutter is 5vw on large screens.

Motion

Animation & Motion

Motion should feel organic and maritime — slow, deliberate, confident. Avoid fast or bouncy animations. All transitions use ease or ease-out curves at 0.2–0.7s durations.

Hover Transitions

0.2–0.3s ease. Subtle lift (translateY -2 to -6px), color shift, or shadow expansion.

Scroll Reveals

0.65s ease. Elements fade up from 24px offset. Use staggered delays (0.1s increments) for grids.

Hero Animations

Background: 18–20s scale drift. Content: 1.1s fade-up on load. Parallax scrolling on bg images.

Components

Buttons & CTAs

All button text uses Jost, uppercase, 700 weight, wide letter-spacing. Buttons are rounded at 4px. The Gold button is the primary booking CTA and should be used sparingly to maintain impact.

Usage hierarchy: Gold = primary CTA (1 per section max) · Navy/Outline = secondary actions · Text link = tertiary / "view more" style actions. Never use two Gold buttons side by side.
Components

Form Elements

Form inputs have 1.5px borders, 4px border-radius, and highlight gold on focus. Labels are uppercase condensed at 0.62rem. Error states use red #c0392b.

Components

Card Patterns

Cards use 8px border-radius, subtle shadows, and hover transforms. Fleet cards use a dark navy background on dark-section contexts; activity/experience cards use cinematic full-bleed backgrounds.

Content

Voice & Tone

Auke Bay Adventures speaks with the confidence of genuine expertise and the warmth of a trusted local guide. We don't oversell — we let Alaska do the selling. Our voice is personal, direct, and unhurried.

✓ Do: Write Like This

  • Use active, direct language that paints a picture
  • "Alaska on your terms" — emphasize autonomy and privacy
  • "Where the only clock is the midnight sun" — evocative, earned poetry
  • Reference real places: Glacier Bay, Inside Passage, Auke Bay Harbor
  • Speak to the guest's aspiration, not just the product
  • Short sentences for impact. Longer ones to build atmosphere.
  • Use "Steve" by name — this is a personal operation, not a corporation

✕ Don't: Avoid These

  • Avoid generic travel-speak ("unforgettable memories await!")
  • Don't over-use exclamation points in body copy
  • Don't use cruise-line language ("amenity-rich," "curated journey")
  • Don't write passive voice — "charters are available" → "we offer charters"
  • Never use superlatives without substance to back them up
  • Avoid long run-on sentences in UI copy or CTAs
  • Don't refer to guests as "passengers" — they're adventurers, anglers, explorers

"If you enjoy the ocean, whales, nature on its own terms, and some of the finest fishing you will ever experience — you owe it to yourself to experience it from the deck of a private yacht where the only clock is the midnight sun."

Imagery Guidelines

Photography must feel genuine, not stock. Real guests, real catches, real Alaska. Dark overlays maintain text readability. All images should have descriptive alt text for SEO and accessibility.

✓ Do Use

Real guest photos · Actual catch photography · Genuine wildlife moments · Authentic Alaska scenery · Behind-the-scenes captain/crew shots

✕ Avoid

Generic stock photography · Overly posed/stiff images · Images from non-Alaska locations · Highly filtered or heavily edited photos that lose authenticity

SEO & Metadata Standards

Every page should be optimized for the keywords guests use to find us. Below are the core standards for all website pages.

Page Title Formula

[Page Name] | Auke Bay Adventures — [City], Alaska

Max 60 characters. Include "Juneau, Alaska" or "Alaska" in every title.

Meta Description Formula

120–155 characters. Include primary keyword, location (Juneau, Alaska), and a CTA. Always include phone number or booking action.

Primary Keywords

Alaska yacht charter Juneau fishing charter Inside Passage charter bareboat charter Alaska Nordic Tug charter Glacier Bay tour Juneau

Image Alt Text

Format: [Subject] [action/context] — [location/brand]
Example: "Halibut fishing charter Juneau Alaska — Auke Bay Adventures"
